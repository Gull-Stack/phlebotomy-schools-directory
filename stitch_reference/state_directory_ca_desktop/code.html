California State Requirements

California maintains the highest standards for phlebotomy in the US. Licensing is managed by the California Department of Public Health (CDPH), Laboratory Field Services (LFS).

CPT I: Certified Phlebotomy Technician I

The industry standard for clinical practice in California.

Required

Academic Prerequisites

  • check_circle High School Diploma or GED Equivalent
  • check_circle Completion of a CDPH-Approved Training Program

Clinical Requirements

  • check_circle 40 hours of basic & advanced classroom instruction
  • check_circle 40 hours of practical clinical experience
  • check_circle 50 venipunctures and 10 skin punctures
military_tech

CPT II Certification

Reserved for experienced professionals. Requires CPT I status plus an additional 20 arterial punctures in a clinical setting under supervision.

Is your school approved?

Only schools listed by Laboratory Field Services (LFS) can qualify you for the state license exam. Do not enroll in non-accredited programs.
